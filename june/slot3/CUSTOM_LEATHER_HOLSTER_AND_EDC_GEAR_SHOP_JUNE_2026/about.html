About HIDE

Craftsmanship Rooted in Tradition, Built for Modern Carry

Our Story

HIDE was born in a small Bozeman workshop in 2014, when founder and master leatherworker Elijah Tanner decided to combine his two passions: traditional leather craftsmanship and the everyday carry community.

What started as custom holsters for local law enforcement grew into a full atelier serving competitive shooters, outdoor enthusiasts, and everyday carriers across the country. Every piece still begins and ends in that same workshop, touched by the same hands that started it all.

Our mission is straightforward: build the finest leather carry gear in America, one piece at a time, with zero shortcuts. We source our hides from American tanneries, hand-select every piece, and refuse to ship anything that doesn't meet our 12-point quality standard.

Our Values

Quality Standards

Our 12-point inspection ensures every piece meets the HIDE standard

Material Sourcing

Every hide is hand-selected from certified American tanneries. We inspect for grain consistency, thickness uniformity, and natural character before accepting any material into our workshop.

Durability Testing

Retention testing, snap stress tests, and stitching pull-strength verification. We simulate years of daily carry in our QA process to ensure your gear performs from day one.

Finish Inspection

Burnished edges, even dye application, aligned stitching, and proper hardware seating. Every detail is examined under magnification before the piece earns our maker's mark.

Our Custom Order Process

From your gear dimensions to a finished, hand-molded piece — six deliberate steps

Step 1

Upload Dimensions

Share photos and measurements of your gear. Our team reviews every detail to ensure a precision fit from day one.

Step 2

Select Your Leather

Choose from our curated selection of full-grain, vegetable-tanned, and exotic leathers. Each hide is hand-inspected for quality.

Step 3

Approve the Sketch

We send you a detailed design sketch showing your holster or pouch. Request revisions until it's exactly what you want.

Step 4

Production Begins

Your piece enters our workshop. Cutting, wet-molding, stitching, and hardware installation — all done by hand.

Step 5

Quality Inspection

Every piece undergoes a 12-point quality check. We test fit, retention, stitching integrity, and edge finish before approval.

Step 6

Delivery to You

Your gear ships in protective packaging with a leather care kit and lifetime craftsmanship warranty card included.

Leather Types Deep Dive

Understanding the character, care, and aging of each leather we work with

Full grain leather is the highest quality leather available. The entire grain surface is preserved, retaining natural markings, texture variations, and the tight fiber structure that gives it exceptional strength.

Aging: Develops a rich patina over months of use. Colors deepen and the leather takes on a personalized character unique to your wear patterns.

Maintenance: Condition every 3-6 months with a quality leather conditioner. Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight and excessive moisture. Store in a cool, dry place when not in use.

Best For: IWB and OWB holsters, duty belts, and heirloom-quality EDC pouches.

Tanned using natural plant tannins (oak bark, mimosa, chestnut), this centuries-old process produces firm, moldable leather ideal for wet-molding holsters. It starts with a light tan color that transforms dramatically over time.

Aging: The most dramatic patina of any leather type. Starts pale and develops deep honey, caramel, and eventually rich brown tones. Sun exposure accelerates darkening.

Maintenance: Apply neatsfoot oil sparingly every 4-6 months. Water spots add character but can be minimized with a light wax finish.

Best For: Wet-molded holsters, knife sheaths, and tooled leather projects.

Originally developed for horse tack, bridle leather is heavily waxed and stuffed with grease during tanning. The result is an incredibly durable, water-resistant leather with a distinctive waxy bloom on the surface.

Aging: The initial wax bloom fades with handling, revealing a deep, lustrous surface beneath. Develops a polished patina with regular use.

Maintenance: Minimal care needed — the heavy wax content provides natural protection. Buff with a soft cloth to restore shine. Re-wax annually for exposed-carry gear.

Best For: Gun belts, magazine carriers, and heavy-use EDC pouches.

Our exotic leathers are sourced from certified farms and tanneries. Ostrich leather features distinctive quill bumps and is remarkably soft yet durable. Alligator leather offers a classic scale pattern and is one of the most prized materials in leathercraft.

Aging: Exotic leathers age gracefully with minimal change in pattern. Colors may deepen slightly, and the natural oils in ostrich leather keep it supple for decades.

Maintenance: Use only exotic-specific conditioners. Keep away from harsh chemicals and excessive moisture. Professional cleaning recommended annually for alligator pieces.

Best For: Premium show holsters, executive EDC accessories, and collector-grade pieces.

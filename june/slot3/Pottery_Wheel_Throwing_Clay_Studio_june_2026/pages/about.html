Our Story

From a Garage to a Community

KILN started in 2018 with a single used wheel in a drafty garage and a deep passion for the therapeutic nature of clay.

Our founder, realizing how pottery provided a grounding escape from an increasingly digital world, wanted to share that experience. What began as informal weekend workshops quickly blossomed as the community grew.

Today, KILN is a 3,000 square foot haven for creators of all levels. We believe that everyone has an innate desire to make things with their hands, and clay is the perfect medium for that expression. We're proud to have guided thousands of students from their first lopsided bowl to confident, independent ceramicists.

Our Values

Craftsmanship

We honor traditional techniques while encouraging modern exploration and personal style.

Community

Creativity

We believe there are no mistakes in clay, only unexpected learning opportunities and new paths.

The Journey of Clay

From raw earth to finished art, the ceramic process requires patience.

1. Preparation

Wedging raw clay to remove air bubbles

2. Shaping

Throwing or hand-building the form

3. Drying

Trimming and drying to leather hard

4. Bisque Fire

First firing hardens the piece

5. Glazing

Applying liquid glass surface decoration

6. Final Fire

High heat melts glaze to finished state

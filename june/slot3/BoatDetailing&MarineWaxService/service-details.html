Marine Ceramic Coating

Premium 9H hardness nano-technology that chemically bonds to your vessel's gel coat, delivering unmatched gloss and hydrophobic protection.

Vessel Protection Re-imagined

Traditional wax breaks down under harsh saltwater and UV exposure in a matter of weeks. Our Marine Ceramic Coating uses state-of-the-art silica (SiO2) nano-particles that penetrate and seal the microscopic pores of your boat's gel coat. Once cured, it creates an semi-permanent, glass-like shield that repels water, blocks UV rays, and stops salt oxidation in its tracks.

UV Damage Defense

Prevents fading, yellowing, and chalking of gel coat surfaces caused by intense solar exposure.

Hydrophobic Effect

Water sheets off instantly, carrying dirt, salt crystals, and soot away. Cleaning becomes effortless.

3–5 Year Durability

Requires no reapplications or heavy seasonal compounding. Simple maintenance keeps the shine alive.

Scratch Resistance

The cured 9H hard layer protects against minor abrasions, docking scuffs, and marine pollutants.

Our Multi-Stage Application Process

Proper surface preparation is 90% of a ceramic coating's success. We adhere to a strict preparation protocol to guarantee molecular bonding.

01

Decontamination Wash

Deep mechanical and chemical cleaning of the hull to strip old wax, salt residues, algae, and organic contaminants. We use specialized fallout removers and clay bars for a completely clean finish.

02

Paint & Gel Coat Correction

Any surface imperfections will be sealed under the coating. We perform a multi-stage machine compound and polish to eliminate oxidation, scratches, and water spots, restoring the mirror reflection.

03

Alcohol Wipe & Surface Prep

We wipe down the entire hull using an Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA) solution. This strips away any remaining polishing oils, ensuring the gel coat is perfectly raw and ready for bonding.

04

Ceramic Coating Application

Our IDA-certified installers apply the liquid ceramic in small, overlapping sections. After flashing, the excess is leveled and hand-buffed using premium microfiber cloths.

05

Curing Process

The coating requires initial curing time (typically 12–24 hours) out of the water. We supervise this process to ensure the ceramic sets to maximum hardness.

Ceramic Coating FAQs

How is ceramic coating different from a regular wax?
Wax sits loosely on top of the gel coat and washes away quickly when exposed to UV and saltwater (usually in 1–3 months). Ceramic coatings physically and chemically bond to the molecular structure of the hull surface, creating a hard, resilient barrier that cannot be washed off and lasts 3–5 years.
Do I still need to wash my boat after it is ceramic coated?
Yes, you must still wash your vessel, but the process is dramatically easier. Dirt, grime, and saltwater spots cannot stick to the hydrophobic coating, so a simple rinse with fresh water and a mild, pH-neutral soap is usually all that's required. You will never need to buff or wax the boat again during the coating's lifespan.
Can ceramic coating be applied to aluminum or teak?
We use specialized ceramic coatings for different boat surfaces. We have glass-specific coatings, metal coatings (for stainless steel rails and aluminum hulls), and upholstery coatings. While teak is not treated with standard ceramic coating, we apply specialized teak sealers that provide similar protective properties.
